# CMPT 419 D200 Syllabus - Fall 2025

**Instructor:** Nicholas Vincent
**Term:** Fall 2025

## Course Structure

### Meeting Format

- **Mondays (1 hour):** Discuss prior week's readings, introduce new materials, begin lecture content
- **Thursdays (2 hours):** Conclude lectures, facilitate discussions, conduct lab/assignment work

The course emphasizes reading and discussion, organized into four sequential modules spanning 13 weeks.

## Course Modules

### Module 1: Frameworks (Weeks 1-4)
Administration and frameworks for human-centered or data-centered approaches to AI.

### Module 2: Technical Foundations (Weeks 5-7)
Technical work in data valuation, scaling, and algorithmic collective action.

### Module 3: Platforms & Ecosystems (Weeks 8-10)
Online platforms, content ecosystems, and data.

### Module 4: Governance Frontiers (Weeks 11-13)
Data governance frontiers including voting and markets.

## Assessment

| Component | Weight | Details |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Reading responses | 10% | 12 submissions; lowest 2 dropped |
| Coding assignments | 20% | 4 assignments (5% each); lowest 1 dropped |
| Quizzes | 20% | 2 quizzes (10% each) |
| Final project | 50% | 5% proposal + 45% execution |

## Reading Assignments

- Posted Mondays of preceding week
- Due Monday of target week before class
- Include mandatory and optional readings
- Submission via Coursys with minimal grading
- AI assistance strongly discouraged for reading responses

## Key Policies

### Participation
Attendance strongly encouraged. All students must contribute to class discussion at least once. Cold-calling used to facilitate this expectation.

### AI Usage
Generative AI permitted with full disclosure:
- For reading responses: strongly avoid AI assistance
- For other work: document all outputs with model identification and editing level
- Individual assignments may impose additional restrictions

### Group Work
Allowed with required contribution statements. All members must review team outputs.

### Programming
Multiple languages supported (Python, JavaScript encouraged). Free tools always available.

### Course Materials
Digitally provided. No single textbook. Curated research papers and book chapters.
