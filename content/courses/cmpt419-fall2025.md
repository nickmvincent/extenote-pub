# CMPT 419 - Collective Action and Public Interest AI (Fall 2025)

This course explores the intersection of collective action, public interest considerations, and artificial intelligence systems.

## Course Structure

The course emphasizes reading and discussion, organized into four sequential modules spanning 13 weeks.

### Course Modules

1. **Module 1 (Weeks 1-4):** Administration and frameworks for human-centered or data-centered approaches
2. **Module 2 (Weeks 5-7):** Technical work in data valuation, scaling, and algorithmic collective action
3. **Module 3 (Weeks 8-10):** Online platforms, content ecosystems, and data
4. **Module 4 (Weeks 11-13):** Data governance frontiers including voting and markets

## Meeting Format

- **Mondays (1 hour):** Discuss prior week's readings, introduce new materials, begin lecture content
- **Thursdays (2 hours):** Conclude lectures, facilitate discussions, conduct lab/assignment work

## Assessment

| Component | Weight | Details |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Reading responses | 10% | 12 submissions; lowest 2 dropped |
| Coding assignments | 20% | 4 assignments; lowest 1 dropped |
| Quizzes | 20% | 2 quizzes (10 points each) |
| Final project | 50% | 5% proposal + 45% execution |

## Key Policies

- **Participation:** Attendance strongly encouraged; all students must contribute to class discussion
- **AI Usage:** Generative AI permitted with full disclosure; avoid AI for reading responses
- **Group Work:** Allowed with required contribution statements
